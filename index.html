MindTap

MindTap is an accessible neurotechnology application that empowers people with physical disabilities to interact with their mobile devices using EEG brain signals.

About MindTap

Many mobile applications assume that users can rely on touch, fine motor control, or voice input. For individuals with physical disabilities, these assumptions can create significant barriers to digital independence.

MindTap was created to challenge those assumptions. By leveraging electroencephalography (EEG) headsets, the application enables hands-free interaction with smartphones, offering an alternative pathway for control that does not depend on physical movement.

Accessibility is not an add-on in MindTap â€” it is foundational. The project follows person-first language, inclusive design practices, and accessibility standards to ensure the experience is usable, respectful, and empowering for a diverse range of users.

The Project

MindTap combines hardware, software, and thoughtful design to create a practical and ethical neurotechnology solution.

  • EEG-based interaction: Brain signal patterns are interpreted and mapped to mobile inputs, allowing users to navigate and interact without touch.
  • Accessible interface design: The application supports screen readers, keyboard navigation, high-contrast visuals, and reduced-motion preferences.
  • Privacy-conscious approach: The project emphasizes responsible data handling and transparency when working with sensitive neural data.
  • Real-world motivation: The design decisions are guided by real accessibility challenges, not hypothetical use cases.

Why It Matters

Assistive technology has the potential to restore autonomy, confidence, and connection. MindTap aims to contribute to that future by exploring how neurotechnology can be applied thoughtfully and inclusively.

This project represents a step toward more equitable digital experiences, where innovation prioritizes people first.
